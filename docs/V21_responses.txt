What does Wolfsberg say about risk-based approach?

The Wolfsberg Group holds that the application of a risk-based approach (RBA) is critical for the effective design and maintenance of a financial institution’s (FIs) financial crime risk management programme, covering its risk appetite, policies, measures, and controls. Core Principles:

Prioritisation: RBA is considered important for the effectiveness and efficiency of the fight against money laundering, allowing FIs to prioritise their effort and activity based on the likelihood of money laundering. (15. Wolfsberg_RBA_Guidance_)
Proportionality: FIs must ensure that their internal controls are proportionately aligned to the risks posed by the range of their clients, where the highest risk clients will be the subject of the most rigorous AML controls—whether through on-boarding standards, enhanced due diligence, enhanced monitoring, and/or more frequent periodic reviews. (17. Wolfsberg-Risk-Assessment-FAQs-2015)
Alignment with FATF: In line with the Financial Action Task Force (FATF), the Group considers an RBA to mean that countries, "competent authorities and FIs should be expected to identify, assess and understand the financial" [incomplete quote in source]. (Wolfsberg_RiskBasedApproach_Statement_July2025 (2)) Limitations:
Regarding terrorist financing, the Wolfsberg Group observes that "a risk based approach... will not generally provide any utility in specifically identifying potential terrorist funds" because distinguishing terrorist funds from other funds is difficult; funds used to finance terrorism do not necessarily derive from criminal activity. (15. Wolfsberg_RBA_Guidance_(2006))
